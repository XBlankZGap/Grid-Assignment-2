THREE TYPES OF CYBER ATTACKS


ESPIONAGE

APT 10 CHINA

A Chinese-backed hacking group infiltrated global IT service providers, using supply chain attacks to steal sensitive data from corporations and governments worldwide, affecting industries like aerospace, telecommunications, and finance.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2018


APT 28 RUSSIA

A Russian military intelligence-linked group hacked the U.S. Democratic National Committee (DNC), leaking emails to influence the presidential election. It also targeted NATO, European governments, and journalists for cyber espionage..

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2016


APT 29 COZY BEAR

A Russian intelligence-backed hacking group infiltrated U.S. government agencies, political organizations, conducting long-term espionage by stealing sensitive documents and compromising high-level communication channels.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2016


SOLAR WINDS ATTACK

A sophisticated cyber espionage compromised SolarWinds' Orion software, allowing Russian hackers to infiltrate U.S. government agencies and major corporations, exfiltrating sensitive data through a trusted software supply chain attack.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2020


MALWARE

STUXNET

A sophisticated worm allegedly developed by the U.S. and Israel to sabotage Iranâ€™s nuclear program by targeting Siemens industrial control systems, causing physical damage to centrifuges while remaining undetected for years.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2010


DUQU

A cyber malware, designed to steal industrial control system data. It gathered intelligence for future attacks, targeting governments and manufacturers, with sophisticated stealth capabilities to evade detection.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2011


FLAME

A highly complex and advanced malware used for intelligence gathering in the Middle East. It could record keystrokes, capture screenshots, activate microphones, and spread via local networks.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2012


NOTPETYA

A destructive wiper malware targeting Ukrainian infrastructure but spread globally. It encrypted files irreversibly, causing billions in damages to multinational corporations and disrupting global supply chains.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2017


RANSOMWARE

CONTI

A ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) group that targeted corporations, governments, and healthcare systems, encrypting data and demanding ransom. Known for double extortion tactics, it leaked stolen data if victims refused to pay.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2020


DARKSIDE

A Russian-linked ransomware attack that shut down the Colonial Pipeline, causing fuel shortages in the U.S. The hackers demanded millions in Bitcoin, leading to heightened cybersecurity measures and government intervention.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2021


REVIL

A notorious ransomware group targeting global businesses, using double extortion by encrypting files and threatening to leak stolen data. It attacked major companies like JBS Foods and Kaseya, demanding multi-million-dollar ransoms.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2020


WANNACRY

A global ransomware attack exploiting an NSA-leaked vulnerability (EternalBlue), rapidly spreading across 150+ countries. It affected hospitals, businesses, and government agencies, encrypting files and demanding Bitcoin ransom, but had a kill switch that slowed its impact.

YEAR OF ATTACK: 2017
